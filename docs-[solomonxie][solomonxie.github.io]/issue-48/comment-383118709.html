Determinant of Transformation

It’s quite easy to calculate, and not too hard to understand what’s behind it. > The Determinant of a transformation is How much the AREA of the new Graph scaled.

JUST TO REMEMBER: THE DETERMINANT IS ABOUT AREA OF THE GRAPH!

Refer to 3Blue1Brown: The determinant

Unit vector graph

We all know the unit vector i & j made an area of 1. But when we do a Linear transformation to the unit vector graph, the area is not 1 anymore, might be bigger or smaller. So how much it re-sized we call it the determinant.

Note that: - Since a/1 = a, so calculating the Area of the new unit vector graph, is equal to the scalar itself. - Calculating how much the unit vector graph scaled, is exactly equal to how much the whole graph scaled.

Zero determinant

If the determinant of a transformation det(M) = 0, then it means the Transformation squishes the graph to a line or a point!

Negative determinant

A negative determinant means the graph has been flipped over by the transformation. Then the j unit vector flip over to the LEFT side of i unit vector.
